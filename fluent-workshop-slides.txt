T∞)```q: Heat transfer rate [W]h: Heat transfer coefficient [W/(m²·K)]A: Surface area [m²]Ts: Surface temperature [K]T∞: Fluid temperature [K]Key InsightIncrease A: Add fins (surface area enhancement)Increase h: Higher velocity, turbulence, better geometryReduce (Ts - T∞): Better heat sink designCFD ConnectionCFD calculates h automatically based on flow physicsVisualizes temperature distributions (Ts variations)Enables geometric optimization for maximum A/Volume ratio---Slide 7: The Problem Setup (Your Challenge)Geometry Configuration```Compartment 1          Compartment 2(Passive Devices)      (Active Devices)    q ≈ 0 W               q = 40 W┌─────────────┬─────────────────┐│             │                 │  ← Air flow (4 m/s, 35°C)│             │     Heat        ││             │   Generation    │└─────────────┴─────────────────┘    36 cm total base plate```What You'll ModelBaseline: Flat bottom heat sinkEnhanced: Rectangular fin heat sinkOptimized: Triangular fin heat sinkThe Discovery ProcessSimulate → Analyze → Discover inadequacy → Redesign → Optimize---Slide 8: Day 1 Agenda - Why It MattersKey Quality MetricsSkewness: <0.85 (max), <0.35 (average)Orthogonal Quality: >0.15 (minimum)Aspect Ratio: Reasonable for boundary layersCell Count: <512,000 for Student versionBoundary Layer Mesh Critical for Heat Transfery+ values: 30-300 for wall functionsInflation layers: 5-10 layers, growth rate 1.2Surface resolution: Fine mesh on heat transfer surfacesWhat Happens with Bad Mesh?Inaccurate heat transfer coefficientsPoor convergence or divergenceUnphysical results (negative temperatures)Wasted computational time---Slide 11: CFD Solution StrategySolver ConfigurationType: Pressure-based, steady-stateTurbulence: k-ε Realizable (external flow)Energy: Enabled (critical for heat transfer!)Materials: Air (temperature-dependent), AluminumBoundary Conditions SetupVelocity Inlet: 4 m/s, 35°C, 5% turbulence intensityPressure Outlet: Atmospheric, backflow temp 35°CHeat Generation: 40W volumetric source in Compartment 2Conjugate Interfaces: Coupled (no thermal resistance)Convergence MonitoringResiduals: Energy <10⁻⁶, others <10⁻⁴Surface monitors: Average temperature on base plateHeat balance: Total heat transfer ≈ 40W---Slide 12: The Discovery MomentWhat You'll Find with Flat PlateAverage temperature: ~90-100°CMaximum temperature: >100°CEngineering conclusion: THERMAL FAILUREElectronics safe limit: <70-80°CWhy Does It Fail?Limited surface area: Only 0.1296 m²Low heat transfer coefficient: ~20-40 W/(m²·K) typical for airInadequate heat dissipation: q = hA(Ts-T∞) insufficientThe Engineering ResponseNeed more surface area: Add finsBut which fin geometry? Let's find out through simulation!This Motivates Real EngineeringNot theoretical exercise - actual thermal failureDrives authentic need for design improvementMirrors industrial problem-solving Troubleshooting GuideCommon Day 1 IssuesMesh fails to generate: Check geometry watertight, reduce element sizePoor mesh quality: Add local refinement at sharp cornersCell count >512k: Increase global element sizeNo inflation layers: Check surface selections, reduce first layer heightCommon Day 2 IssuesSolution diverges: Check boundary conditions, reduce relaxation factorsPoor
